How to Support a Friend

Published on October 20, 2023

Starting the Conversation

It can be difficult to approach a friend you're worried about. The key is to come from a place of care and concern, not judgment. Start by finding a private, comfortable time to talk. Use "I" statements to express your feelings, such as, "I've been worried about you lately," rather than accusatory "you" statements.

Listen Without Judgment

One of the most powerful things you can do is listen. Allow your friend to share their experience without interrupting or offering unsolicited advice. Validate their feelings by saying things like, "That sounds really difficult." Your goal is to create a safe space where they feel heard and understood.

Encourage, Don't Force

You can't force someone to seek help, but you can encourage them. Offer to help them find resources, such as the campus helpline or a local support group. You could say, "Would you be open to talking to someone who can help? I can sit with you while you make the call." Supporting them in taking the first step can make all the difference.
