Understanding Addiction

Presented by

About this Webinar

In this video, Dr. Anna Lembke, a leading psychiatrist and addiction specialist at Stanford University, explains how addiction affects the brain and behavior. She discusses how everyday substances and activities—like drugs, alcohol, social media, or gaming—can trigger the brain’s reward system, leading to dependency. Dr. Lembke emphasizes that addiction is not just a moral issue but a medical condition that can be treated with awareness, therapy, and lifestyle balance. She encourages students to build healthy habits, seek support when needed, and understand the importance of a balanced “dopamine diet” — learning to find joy in real-life connections, creativity, and purpose rather than quick digital or chemical highs. This message aligns with our Drug-Free Campus initiative, which aims to educate students about addiction, promote mental well-being, and create a supportive, substance-free environment where everyone can thrive.

Viewers will gain insights into the latest research and learn to identify early warning signs in themselves and others.
